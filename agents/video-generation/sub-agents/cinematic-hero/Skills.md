# Skills — Cinematic Hero Specialist

## 1. Visual Storytelling Without Dialogue
- Show, don't tell — visual metaphors over spoken explanation
- Movement as narrative: slow reveal, pull-back, push-in, orbit
- Texture and material as character (glass, water, light, fabric)
- Music/sound design as the primary emotional driver

## 2. Shot Composition for Cinema
- Anamorphic wide shots for establishing (2.39:1 feel even in 16:9)
- Shallow DOF portraits for intimate product moments
- Slow motion for hero details (3x speed, liquid, fabric, light)
- Symmetry and geometric framing for premium authority

## 3. Color Grading Direction
- Teal and orange: Cinematic blockbuster
- Desaturated warm: Premium lifestyle (Aesop, Muji)
- High contrast monochrome: Bold editorial
- Golden hour warm: Aspirational outdoor
- Cool blue-silver: Tech/innovation

## 4. Narrative Arc (Non-Verbal)
- Opening: Environment establishing, mood setting
- Rising: Product introduction, detail discovery
- Climax: The hero moment — product fully revealed in glory
- Resolution: Brand lockup, aspiration, emotional landing

## 5. Transition Language
- Fade through black (gravitas)
- Match cut (visual rhyming between shots)
- Light flare transition (premium, cinematic)
- Slow dissolve (dreamlike, emotional)
