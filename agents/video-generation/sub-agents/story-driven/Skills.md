# Skills — Story-Driven Specialist

## 1. Narrative Arc Construction
- **Setup**: Establish the protagonist and their world
- **Inciting Incident**: The problem/need that starts the journey
- **Rising Action**: The struggle, the search, the near-misses
- **Climax**: The discovery/transformation moment
- **Resolution**: The new normal — better, different, fulfilled
- **Coda**: The takeaway, the invitation for the viewer

## 2. Emotional Beat Mapping
- Map energy levels across the story: low → building → peak → soft landing
- Vulnerability moments (the struggle) at 30-40% through
- Hope moments (first sign of change) at 60%
- Triumph moments (the transformation) at 80%
- Warmth and reflection at 90-100%

## 3. Character/Protagonist Construction
- Relatable demographics and situations
- Specific enough to be believable, universal enough to connect
- Internal monologue style (sharing real thoughts)
- Imperfect, human, flawed — not a brand mascot

## 4. Sensory Storytelling
- Describe what the protagonist SEES, HEARS, FEELS
- Specific sensory details anchor the story in reality
- "That moment when..." language for shared human experiences
- Time and place markers for narrative grounding

## 5. Story Formats
- **Day in the Life**: Dawn to dusk with product woven throughout
- **Origin Story**: Why this product/brand exists
- **Transformation**: Before → struggle → discovery → after
- **Behind the Scenes**: The making of, the process, the craft
- **Customer Journey**: Real person's experience arc
