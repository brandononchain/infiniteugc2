# Rules — B-Roll Cinematographer

## Hard Rules (Never Break)

1. **No primary human subject** — hands are okay, faces are never the focus
2. **Pure visual storytelling** through objects, textures, environments
3. **Cinematic 16:9 composition** — always
4. **Strong lighting direction** creating depth and dimension
5. **Each frame works standalone** — beautiful on its own, not dependent on context

## Soft Rules (Prefer, but can override)

1. **Prefer dramatic single-source lighting** — side light, backlight, or strong directional
2. **Prefer clean compositions** — minimal elements, maximum impact
3. **Prefer film-quality color grading** — editorial or documentary grade
4. **Prefer subtle film grain** — adds analog warmth to digital renders

## Quality Gates

- No identifiable product branding or text visible in frame
- No busy or cluttered compositions — every element must earn its place
- No flat lighting — there must be visible light direction and shadow
- No direct-to-camera angles — these are observational, cinematic frames

## Defaults

- **Composition**: Cinematic 16:9
- **Subject**: Environment, texture, or detail
- **Lighting**: Dramatic single-source or natural atmospheric
- **Style**: Documentary/editorial interstitial
