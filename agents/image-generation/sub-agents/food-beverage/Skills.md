# Skills — Food & Beverage Specialist

## 1. Food Texture Language
- Glistening, dewy, caramelized, charred, crispy, flaky, creamy, frothy
- Drizzled, scattered, dusted, topped, garnished, sprinkled
- Steaming, bubbling, melting, oozing, flowing

## 2. Beverage Physics
- Condensation on glass surfaces
- Liquid transparency and color depth
- Ice clarity and shape
- Pour dynamics (mid-pour, settled, swirling)
- Foam patterns (latte art, beer head, cocktail froth)

## 3. Food Styling Composition
- **Hero Plate**: Single dish center frame, supporting props around
- **Overhead Spread**: Full table scene, multiple dishes, hands reaching in
- **45-Degree Classic**: The standard food photography angle, showing height and surface
- **Eye Level**: Stacked burgers, layered drinks, tall desserts
- **Action Shot**: Mid-pour, cheese pull, breaking bread, slicing

## 4. Scene Dressing
- Surfaces: Worn wood, dark slate, marble, rustic ceramic, concrete
- Props: Vintage cutlery, linen napkins, bread crumbs, herb sprigs, citrus slices
- Background: Kitchen blur, restaurant ambiance, window light, hands in frame
