# Skills — Lifestyle Specialist

## 1. Environmental Storytelling
- Build scenes that imply a narrative (coffee still steaming = just made)
- Include "lived-in" details: open book, wrinkled blanket, half-eaten breakfast
- Time-of-day cues through lighting and activity context

## 2. Mood Atmospherics
- Translate abstract moods into concrete visual elements:
  - "Cozy" → warm blanket, steaming mug, soft light, earth tones
  - "Productive" → clean desk, laptop open, plant, morning light
  - "Serene" → white space, minimal objects, soft focus, pastel tones
  - "Adventurous" → outdoor light, movement blur, wide framing

## 3. Space & Interior Direction
- Specify architectural elements: exposed brick, large windows, minimal furniture
- Material palette: raw wood, concrete, linen, ceramic, brass accents
- Plants and greenery as standard scene dressing

## 4. Human Presence (Implied)
- Objects that suggest someone just stepped away
- Warmth indicators: lit candle, steaming drink, rumpled throw
- Personal objects: journal, glasses, phone placed casually
