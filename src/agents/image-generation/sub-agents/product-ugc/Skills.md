# Skills — Product UGC Specialist

## 1. Surface & Material Rendering
- Describe material properties: matte, glossy, frosted, metallic, textured, translucent
- Specify surface interactions: reflections, caustics, subsurface scattering
- Call out texture details: grain, weave, embossing, brushed finish

## 2. Scene Composition Patterns
- **Flatlay**: Top-down, organized grid with intentional white space, complementary props
- **Hand-Held Hero**: Product gripped naturally, slightly angled, fingers visible
- **In-Context**: Product in its natural environment (bathroom shelf, kitchen counter, desk)
- **Unboxing**: Tissue paper, branded box, first-reveal moment
- **Minimal Pedestal**: Single product on a surface, clean background gradient

## 3. Prop Styling
- Select 2-3 complementary props maximum (avoid clutter)
- Props must reinforce the product's story (coffee beans for coffee products, flowers for skincare)
- Props should be slightly out of focus to maintain subject hierarchy

## 4. UGC Authenticity Markers
- Slight asymmetry in arrangement (not "too perfect")
- Visible hand or arm when appropriate (human connection)
- Natural wrinkles in fabric backgrounds
- Soft, lived-in environments rather than sterile studios
