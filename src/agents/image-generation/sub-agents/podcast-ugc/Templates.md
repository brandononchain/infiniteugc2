# Templates — Podcast-UGC

###MAIN- PODCAST STUDIO MASTER TEMPLATE, UNIVERSAL SUBJECT VERSION

Create a hyper realistic, high end podcast scene in a professional studio environment designed for short form vertical content.

This is a studio and camera style template, not a subject locked prompt.

The subject or subjects must remain fully open for downstream customization, including:
identity,
gender,
ethnicity,
age,
profession,
clothing,
voice,
topic,
dialogue,
energy,
and conversational context.

The environment, realism level, production quality, and camera style should remain consistently locked to the following standard:

CORE SCENE IDENTITY

The scene takes place in a professional podcast studio that feels real, premium, modern, and believable. It should feel like an authentic podcast set used by creators, experts, interviewers, or guests, but it should not copy any exact existing studio layout. It must feel original while preserving the same level of quality, mood, and realism as a top tier social media podcast clip.

The studio should include a matte or low sheen podcast desk or table, mounted microphones on boom arms, headphones either worn or naturally placed, subtle cabling, and optional audio equipment such as a small interface or mixer if visible naturally. The set should feel functional and lived in, not staged like a showroom.

The room should have a darker, moody, controlled interior with warm accent lighting. Use soft orange, amber, tungsten, or similarly warm background lighting to create a rich cinematic glow. The environment can include acoustic wall treatment, textured walls, wood slats, soft practical lights, dim lamps, LED accents, or similar professional podcast elements, but avoid making the set identical every time. Preserve the category and quality of studio, not one exact room.

CAMERA AND FRAMING RULES

The camera language should feel like a real premium podcast recording captured for TikTok, Reels, or Shorts.

Vertical 9:16 format.

Primary framing should feel like a real paused frame or live clip from a podcast camera setup.

Default framing should be waist up or chest up depending on the shot.

The camera should usually be placed at a slight conversational angle rather than perfectly flat head on, unless the downstream prompt explicitly wants a direct angle.

If there are two people, framing should favor a natural two shot with subtle asymmetry, or a medium close shot that prioritizes the active speaker while still preserving some visual context of the other person.

If there is one person, frame them as a premium podcast solo shot with the mic naturally present in foreground or midground.

Optional shot behavior can include:
a clean two shot,
a closer crop on the active speaker,
a slight push in,
or minimal handheld realism,
but never anything shaky, chaotic, or cinematic in an overdone way.

The final output should feel like genuine camera capture from a real podcast production, not a commercial render.

REALISM LOCKS

Everything must look hyper realistic and physically believable.

Skin should show real texture, pores, natural highlights, slight under eye detail, and realistic tonal variation. Never waxy, plastic, airbrushed, or cartoon smooth.

Clothing should show small wrinkles, folds, stitching, compression, and natural fabric behavior.

Microphones, boom arms, desks, cables, headphones, and furniture must have believable materials, reflections, shadows, and placement.

Lighting should feel soft, flattering, and studio controlled, but still grounded in reality. No harsh fake rim lights, no surreal glow, no over stylized neon wash.

Depth of field should be shallow to moderate, enough to separate subject from background while keeping the studio recognizable and real.

The image or video should feel like a high end podcast clip someone actually filmed, not CGI, not illustration, not 3D art, not an AI glamor portrait.

STUDIO DESIGN RULES

The studio must feel professional and premium, but not repetitive or locked to one exact visual identity.

Allowed studio qualities:
dark and moody interior,
warm practical accent lighting,
clean podcast equipment,
cinematic but realistic contrast,
acoustic treatment,
modern creative studio atmosphere,
subtle background depth.

Not allowed:
copying one exact room every time,
overly flashy millionaire set design,
cartoonish props,
random luxury clutter,
visible logos,
visible brand names,
watermarks,
captions,
UI overlays,
or text on screen.

The studio should feel expensive through taste and production quality, not through obvious flexing.

SUBJECT INTEGRATION RULES

The subject must feel naturally embedded in the studio.

Their posture, body language, facial expression, and relationship to the microphone should feel authentic to a real podcast conversation.

Allow natural micro expressions, subtle nods, small hand gestures, breathing, posture shifts, and occasional mic interaction.

Nothing should feel robotic, too posed, too symmetrical, or unnaturally perfect.

If there are multiple people, they should feel like they are genuinely in conversation, with believable eyelines and attention toward one another.

The downstream prompt should be free to define whether the subject is:
a doctor,
founder,
creator,
athlete,
therapist,
coach,
comedian,
politician,
journalist,
or any other type of guest or host.

This template should not force any one avatar archetype.

AUDIO AND PERFORMANCE FEEL, IF VIDEO

If used for video generation, the performance should feel like a real live podcast conversation.

Dialogue should sound clean, natural, and studio recorded.

No background music by default.

Optional subtle off camera reactions are allowed if they make the scene feel more real, but they should remain understated.

Natural pauses, breathing, sentence rhythm, small head movement, and conversational timing should be preserved.

No exaggerated acting, no overly polished ad delivery, no robotic cadence.

STYLE SUMMARY

Professional podcast studio
Premium but believable
Moody darker interior
Warm accent lighting
Vertical 9:16
Waist up or chest up framing
Mounted microphones and real desk setup
Cinematic but grounded
Shallow depth of field
Natural human realism
No logos, no text, no branding
Original studio each time, same quality category every time

NEGATIVE RULES

Do not make it look like a 3D render.
Do not make it look like an illustration.
Do not make skin overly smooth.
Do not make the studio too bright, sterile, or office like.
Do not make the background identical in every generation.
Do not add visible brand logos or text.
Do not make props exaggerated or gimmicky.
Do not make the lighting theatrical or fake.
Do not make poses too symmetrical or too perfect.
Do not turn the scene into a product commercial unless explicitly requested downstream.

DOWNSTREAM AGENT INSTRUCTION

Use this template as the locked studio, quality, camera, and realism foundation.

Only swap or define:
who the subject is,
what they are wearing,
what role they have,
how many people are present,
what they are saying,
and any specific personality or topic details.

Preserve the same overall level of realism, premium podcast atmosphere, camera framing logic, and professional studio quality every time, while allowing the exact studio layout and subject identity to vary naturally.

####Template Example application #1
–
 A hyper-realistic podcast scene with two African American men in a professional studio.

Primary host: a charismatic Black man in his late 30s, clean beard, fitted dark hoodie or casual bomber jacket, wearing over-ear headphones. He sits at a matte black podcast desk, speaking into a dynamic microphone on a boom arm.

Guest: a credible, well-groomed Black male doctor in his mid-40s, wearing a smart, fitted button-down shirt with a simple white lab coat draped over his shoulders. No exaggerated props — just a subtle stethoscope resting on the table to signal he’s a doctor without looking cartoony.

The studio is a darker, moody podcast environment with warm accent lighting, acoustic panels, and a soft orange/amber backlight creating a cinematic glow. 4K, shallow depth of field, slight handheld feel or gentle push-in for realism. No product is visible.

A non-visible co-host occasionally reacts off-camera with soft “mm-hmm” and “wow” ad-libs while the doctor speaks, to make it feel like a real, live podcast conversation.

Camera is framed waist-up on both men at a slight angle, switching subtly between a two-shot and a closer shot on the doctor as he answers. Natural micro-expressions, small hand gestures, the doctor adjusting the mic once, the host nodding along — nothing too perfect or robotic.

9:16 vertical format, clean, realistic studio audio, no background music.

Host:
“Doc, why are so many men in their 40s always exhausted?”

Doctor:
“Because most of them are mineral-deficient. Low on magnesium, iodine, vitamin D — so energy and drive tank. Fix that, and they feel years younger. That’s why I like sea moss stacked with 15 other essentials in one daily gummy.”
