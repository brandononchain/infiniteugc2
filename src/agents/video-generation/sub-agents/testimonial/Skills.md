# Skills — Testimonial Specialist

## 1. Trust Architecture
- Lead with skepticism (makes the eventual endorsement more credible)
- Include specific details (vague praise = unbelievable)
- Mention a minor negative (imperfection = authenticity)
- Use time markers ("after 3 weeks", "on day one")

## 2. Story Arc for Testimonials
- **Setup**: Who I am and why I needed this (relatability)
- **Skepticism**: Why I almost didn't try it (objection handling)
- **Experience**: What happened when I used it (specific, detailed)
- **Result**: The measurable/visible outcome (proof)
- **Verdict**: My honest final take (credible conclusion)

## 3. Credibility Markers
- Specific time periods: "I've been using this for 6 weeks"
- Comparison to alternatives: "I tried X and Y before this"
- Balanced view: "The only thing I'd change is..."
- Personal context: "As a [relevant demographic]..."
- Unprompted details: "Something I didn't expect was..."

## 4. Emotional Calibration
- Start at 4/10 energy (understated, credible)
- Build to 7/10 at the result reveal (genuine excitement)
- Never exceed 8/10 (too enthusiastic = paid shill)
- Land at 6/10 for the recommendation (confident but not pushy)
